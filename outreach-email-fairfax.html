Our names are Charles and Marie Martin, and we are Vice Presidents at ModelIt! K-12 — a STEM education initiative focused on bringing computational modeling and simulations into K-12 classrooms. You can see examples of our work on our YouTube channel: youtube.com/@modelitinaction.

We took our family to see Hoppers on opening day and were captivated — not just by the story, but by how much real science was clearly embedded in the film. When we saw your name in the credits as the Science Expert, we knew we had to reach out.

What We Do

ModelIt! builds interactive computational models and digital simulations that make science tangible for K-12 students. We work in collaboration with Dr. Tomáš Helikar at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln, whose research in digital twins and computational modeling — including the Cell Collective platform — is backed by significant NSF and NIH funding. Our role is translating research-grade modeling tools into accessible, standards-aligned experiences that students and teachers can actually use in classrooms.

We are not scientists — we are the bridge between the science and the classroom. That's why we're reaching out to you.

Why Hoppers

Hoppers is exactly the kind of film that makes our work possible. When students are already excited about a story, connecting it to real science through computational models becomes natural. The science behind the film — the ecology, the engineering, the systems thinking — is rich with possibilities for the kinds of interactive modeling lessons we build. But we need the expertise of someone like you to make sure we get the science right.

Timing

We have two equity-focused education conferences this month in Anaheim, California, including the ACSA Equity Institute (March 25-27), attended by California school administrators and education leaders. Having lessons connected to a current Pixar film, grounded in your published research, would be a powerful way to show these administrators how culturally relevant STEM content can reach more students.

What We're Proposing

We would be honored to explore any level of collaboration that feels right to you:

  1. Content review — We develop the computational modeling lessons and you review them for scientific accuracy, with full credit as our science advisor
  2. Co-development — We work together to design lessons that incorporate your research directly into student-facing simulations
  3. Guest content — A short video introduction or written foreword from you that we include with the lesson materials
  4. Speaking/presentation — If you're interested and available, a joint presentation at a future education conference

We are flexible and respectful of your time. Even a brief conversation would mean a great deal to us and, more importantly, to the students who will benefit from this work.
