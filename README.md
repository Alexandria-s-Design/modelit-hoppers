# ModelIt! x Disney Pixar's *Hoppers* — STEM Lesson Collection

## Overview

A collection of K-12 STEM lessons inspired by the science behind Disney Pixar's *Hoppers* (2026). These lessons leverage the film's themes — beaver ecology, consciousness transfer into robotic animals, ecosystem engineering, and human-wildlife communication — to teach real-world science concepts aligned with NGSS standards.

## The Science of *Hoppers*

In *Hoppers*, scientists at Beaverton University discover how to "hop" human consciousness into lifelike robotic animals, allowing people to experience the world as animals do and communicate with wildlife. The film highlights:

- **Beaver Ecology & Ecohydrology** — How beavers engineer ecosystems by altering waterways, creating wetlands, and building drought/fire-resistant landscapes
- **Biomimicry & Robotics** — Designing lifelike robotic animals that replicate real animal behavior and physiology
- **Neuroscience & Consciousness** — The concept of transferring awareness between biological and artificial systems
- **Environmental Science** — Ecosystem restoration, biodiversity, and the cascading effects of keystone species
- **Community & Collaboration** — How animal communities (beaver colonies) model cooperation and shared purpose

## Proposed Lesson Modules

### Module 1: Ecosystem Engineers (Grades 3-5)
- What makes beavers a "keystone species"?
- Build a model beaver dam and observe water flow changes
- NGSS: 3-LS4-3, 3-LS4-4, 5-ESS2-1

### Module 2: Biomimicry — Nature's Blueprints (Grades 4-8)
- How do engineers design robots inspired by real animals?
- Design challenge: create a robotic animal prototype that solves an environmental problem
- NGSS: 3-5-ETS1-1, MS-ETS1-1, MS-LS1-3

### Module 3: Ecohydrology — Follow the Water (Grades 6-8)
- How do beaver dams change water systems and create fire-resistant landscapes?
- Watershed modeling and simulation activity
- NGSS: MS-ESS2-4, MS-ESS3-3, MS-LS2-4

### Module 4: The Brain-Machine Connection (Grades 6-10)
- How does the brain process sensory information differently across species?
- Explore brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) and their real-world applications
- NGSS: MS-LS1-8, HS-LS1-2

### Module 5: Restoring Ecosystems — The Beaver Solution (Grades 8-12)
- Case study: using beaver reintroduction for wildfire prevention and drought resilience
- Data analysis with real research from Dr. Emily Fairfax's studies
- NGSS: HS-LS2-6, HS-LS2-7, HS-ESS3-4

### Module 6: AI, Robotics & Ethics (Grades 9-12)
- If we could "hop" into an animal's experience, should we?
- Ethics of consciousness transfer, animal rights, and technology boundaries
- NGSS: HS-ETS1-3 + interdisciplinary ethics standards

## Science Consultant Connection

The film's credited science expert is **Dr. Emily Fairfax**, an ecohydrologist and assistant professor of geography at the University of Minnesota. Her decade of beaver research directly informed the film's scientific accuracy. A character in the film — Dr. Samantha E. Fairfax — is named in her honor.

We are reaching out to Dr. Fairfax to explore collaboration on lesson content, ensuring scientific accuracy and leveraging her research for student-facing materials.
